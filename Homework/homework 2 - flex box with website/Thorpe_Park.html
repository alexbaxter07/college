History of Thorpe Park

Thorpe Park, located in Chertsey, Surrey, England, opened its doors on April 24, 1979. Originally a country estate parkland, it was advertised as a adventure of leisure. It was transformed into a theme park with modest attractions such as fairground rides and family-friendly experiences. In the early years, the park began to establish itself by gradually expanding its ride offerings and attractions, setting the stage for future growth. In the 80s and 90s when other theme parks were making a name for themselves and gathering thousands of people per day to come and see there attractions Thorpe park remained as a leisure day for the family. It had model world, ww1, aircraft display and more. It was known as a museum rather than a theme park. In the early 80s they had water boats to take you across the park and later on take you across to the park. They then included several small childrens rides in 1982. The 1990s marked a period of significant transformation for Thorpe Park. During this decade, the park began focusing more on thrilling rides, which played a crucial role in its evolution. The introduction of larger and more exhilarating attractions helped distinguish Thorpe Park from other theme parks. A pivotal moment came in 1994 with the opening of Nemesis Inferno, a roller coaster that became a major draw and contributed to the parks growing reputation for high-energy rides. In 2000, Thorpe Park was acquired by the Tussauds Group, which was subsequently acquired by Merlin Entertainments. This change in ownership led to increased investment and development. The park saw the addition of several notable attractions, including Stealth in 2006, a roller coaster that became one of the tallest and fastest in the UK. This addition, along with others like Saw The Ride in 2009, solidified Thorpe Parks status as a premier destination for thrill-seekers. The 2010s were characterized by continued investment in high energy and immersive experiences. The park introduced rides such as Swarm, a roller coaster that further cemented its reputation, and The Walking Dead The Ride, which added a new level of thematic excitement. Now in 2024, They take the Guiness world record off of The Big One at Blackpool Pleasure Beach with their new ride Hyperia opening as Europes tallest roller coaster. Thorpe Park's commitment to staying at the forefront of the theme park industry is evident in its ongoing efforts to refresh and expand its attractions, ensuring it remains a popular choice for both adrenaline enthusiasts and families.

Stealth

The development of Stealth was part of Thorpe Parks strategy to enhance its reputation as a destination for high adrenaline rides. Stealth is a launched roller coaster, which distinguishes it from traditional chain-lift coasters. It features a hydraulic launch system that propels trains from 0 to 80 miles per hour (128 km/h) in just a few seconds. The ride hight makes it one of the tallest and fastest roller coasters in the UK. The coasters layout includes a high speed vertical climb, a near vertical drop, and a series of airtime hills that provide intense thrills and weightlessness. Its design emphasizes rapid acceleration and height, offering a brief but intense experience that is both thrilling and memorable. Stealth officially opened to the public on March 14, 2006.
